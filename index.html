Timeline of Hermès, Louis Vuitton and Lanvin

Today, Hermès, Louis Vuitton and Lanvin stand has the oldest fashion houses still in operation, respectively. Their legacies, while each different, all intertwine with the passion of high craftsmanship and art.

If you have an interest in the histories of these fashion houses, then follow the timeline below to learn about how they developed from 1837 to today.

  • Hermès, 1837In 1837, Thierry Hermès opened the first Hermès store in Paris, which manufactured harnesses for horses. The Hermès logo, today, reflects the fashion house’s roots in harness manufacturing.

  • Louis Vuitton, 1854Founder Louis Vuitton opened his first workshop in 1854 after a 17-year apprenticeship with Monsieur Maréchal, a box and trunk builder. Originally, the company stayed in-line with Vuitton’s skills; it sold luxury boxes and trunks for traveling.

  • Louis Vuitton, 1859After experiencing rapid early growth, Vuitton opened the Asnières workshop in 1859. The workshop is still in use to this day and is where 170 craftsmen make special-order products.

  • Hermès, 18801880 marked a turning point for the fashion house, as Charles-Émile Hermès, Thierry Hermès’ son, opened its flagship store at 24 Faubourg Saint-Honoré, which made tailor-made horse harnesses.

  • Louis Vuitton, 1886In 1886, Louis Vuitton and Georges Vuitton, Louis Vuitton’s son, created and patented a two-spring-buckle-locking system, which is still used on the company’s trunks today.

  • Lanvin, 1889Jeanne Lanvin opened her first store in 1889, which manufactured hats.

  • Louis Vuitton, 1892Louis Vuitton passed away in 1892; ownership of the company fell to his son Georges Vuitton. In tribute to his father, Georges created the LV monogram, a motif found on many of the houses’ goods featuring the brand’s initials along with floral designs.

  • Lanvin, 18971897 was a monumental moment in Jeanne Lanvin’s life, as it was the year of her daughter's, Marguerite, birth. Developing a deep bond with her daughter, Jeanne Lanvin would create her luxurious clothes, attracting the mothers of Marguerite’s friends.

  • Lanvin, 1908In 1908, Jeanne Lanvin opened her first children’s clothing store. Demand was so high for her clothing that she opened a women’s clothing store the next year.

  • Lanvin, 1915Starting in 1915 and ending in 1945, Jeanne Lanvin traveled to shows in San Francisco and Paris to showcase her designs, including the San Francisco International Exposition and the Paris International Exhibition of the Decorative Arts.

  • Hermès, 1922In 1922-28, Émile Hermès, Charles-Émile Hermès’ son, took over the company. Under his management, the fashion house began creating a plethora of items, including leather goods, ready-to-wear men’s clothing and jewelry.

  • Lanvin, 1926In 1926, Lanvin opened the first company’s first line of men’s clothing.

  • Louis Vuitton, 1936In 1936, Gaston Louis-Vuitton, Georges Vuitton’s son, took control of the company after his father’s passing. Under his management, the company ventured into leather goods.

  • Hermès, 19371937 saw the creation of one of the houses’ staples: the silk scarf. Robert Dumas, Émile Hermès son-in-law, created the first silk scarf, Jeu des omnibus et dames blanches.

  • Lanvin, 1946With Jeanne Lanvin’s passing in 1946, the company fell to her daughter Marie-Blanche, who continued her mother’s legacy.

  • Hermès, 1956In 1956, Grace Kelly, an actress and Princess of Monaco, wore one of the houses’ handbags. This handbag became signature of the house and was named after the princess, called the Kelly bag.

  • Louis Vuitton, 19841984 marked a pivotal moment, not only for Louis Vuitton, but for the entire fashion industry. Louis Vuitton partnered with Moët et Chandon and Hennessy to become LVMH, the largest luxury brand in fashion. Today, LVMH is the parent company of well-known brands such as Bulgari, Givenchy and Tiffany & Co.

  • Hermès, 19871987 marked the houses’ 150th anniversary.

  • Hermès, 2024Today, the house continues to focus on creating luxury items with artistic integrity. The house has 294 stores, operating in 45 countries.

  • Louis Vuitton, 2024Today, Louis Vuitton continues to create luxury goods, including clothing, jewelry and accessories, as well as its founding product, luggage.

  • Lanvin, 2024In 2024, Peter Copping, the former head of couture at Balenciaga and head of womenswear at Louis Vuitton, was announced the lead artistic director of Lanvin.
